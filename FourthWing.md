## weathered
- worn by long exposure to the atmosphere; weather-beaten: weathered rock.

## scribe
- noun
  historical a person who copied out documents, especially one employed to do this before printing was invented.
  • often humorous a writer, especially a journalist.
- verb [with object]
  mainly literary write: he scribed a note that he passed to Dan.

## take out
- (take someone or something out, take out someone or something) informal
  kill, destroy, or disable someone or something: some say the Mob took him out for crossing them.
-  (take something out, take out something)
   obtain an official document or service: you can take out a loan for a specific purchase.
- (take someone out, take out someone)
  escort someone to a social event or place of entertainment: I finally get to take her out on Saturday night.

## perilous
- adjective
  full of danger or risk: a perilous journey south.
- exposed to imminent risk of disaster or ruin: the economy is in a perilous state.

## saunter | ˈsôn(t)ər |
- verb [no object, with adverbial of direction]
  walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or effort: Adam sauntered into the room.
- noun
  a leisurely stroll: a quiet saunter down the road.

## gawk | ɡôk
- verb [no object]
  stare openly and stupidly: they were gawking at some pinup.
- noun
  an awkward or shy person: let those country gawks dance to their hearts' content.
## tilt
- verb
- move or cause to move into a sloping position: [no object] : the floor tilted slightly
  | [with object] : he tilted his head to one side.
- change or cause to change in favor of one person or thing as opposed to another:
  [no object] : the balance of industrial power tilted towards the workers.
  [with object] move (a camera) in a vertical plane: tilting the camera causes convergence of upright lines.

## dote | dōt |
verb
- (dote on/upon) [no object] be extremely and uncritically fond of: she doted on her two young children.

## abhorrent
adjective
- inspiring disgust and loathing; repugnant: racial discrimination was abhorrent to us all.
